Susan Stryker

Professor, author, and filmmaker who focuses on gender and human sexuality, speaking on Pauli Murray's legacy and other topics

Date

Wednesday, November 20

Time

3pm — 5pm

Location

Pauli Murray Head of College House, 100 Sachem St

Details

Susan Stryker has been publishing work on transgender and queer culture since the mid-nineties. Her article, “My Words to Victor Frankenstein Above the Village of Chamonix,” was one of the first articles ever published in a peer-reviewed academic journal by an openinly transgender author, and her work since has won multiple Lambda Awards. She won the 2005 San Francisco/Northern California Emmy Award as director for Screaming Queens: The Riot at Compton’s Cafeteria, a documentary about the Gene Compton Cafeteria Riot of 1966. She has been awarded a postdoctoral research fellowship from Stanford and has served as a visiting professor at Harvard, the University of California at Santa Cruz, and Simon Frazer University. She is a Professor of Gender and Women's Studies and founder of the Transgender Studies Initiative at the University of Arizona, and is currently on leave while holding an appointment as Visiting Professor of Women's, Gender, and Sexuality Studies at Yale.
